Core Prompting Techniques

Learn the principles of talking to AI effectively. Click on a tip to learn more.

1. Be Clear & Specific

Give detailed instructions to get tailored results.

Example:

Vague: "Write a story."
Specific: "Write a 3-paragraph short story about a brave squirrel finding a special nut, from the squirrel's perspective, set in a cozy park."

2. Give Context & Role

Tell the AI who to be to shape its tone and knowledge.

Example:

No Role: "Give me advice on saving money."
With Role: "You are a financial advisor for young adults. Give me 3 practical tips for saving money on a tight budget."

3. Specify Format & Length

Ask for the answer in the exact format you need.

Example:

No Format: "Summarize the benefits of exercise."
Formatted: "Summarize the top 5 benefits of exercise in bullet points, each under 10 words."

4. Iterate & Refine

Treat it like a conversation and ask follow-up questions.

Example:

Initial: "Write a polite text to cancel coffee."
Refinement: "That's good. Now make it sound more enthusiastic about rescheduling."

Practical Application Areas

Explore how AI can help in different parts of your life.

AI can help you manage your time, plan tasks, and organize information. Think of it as a super-efficient personal assistant for your daily life.

Try this prompt:

"Generate a daily to-do list for someone working from home, including tasks for work, household chores, and a short break. Organize it by time blocks."

Or this one:

"Help me decide between making pasta or ordering pizza for dinner tonight. List 3 pros and 3 cons for each."

Beyond just drafting, AI can help you brainstorm communication strategies, overcome writer's block, or adapt your tone for any situation.

Try this prompt for a tricky message:

"Write a polite text message to my friend canceling our coffee plans for tomorrow, but suggest rescheduling for next week."

Or for a creative spark:

"Write a fun and quirky birthday message for a friend who loves cats and reading."

AI is like a personal tutor or a super-fast research assistant. It can explain complex topics simply, summarize long texts, or give you quick facts.

Try this prompt:

"Explain the concept of 'supply and demand' in simple terms, like you're talking to a curious 10-year-old."

Your AI Journey: Important Considerations

Key points to keep in mind as you integrate AI into your daily life.

🤖 AI is a Tool, Not a Human

Before you use AI, how do you think it 'learns' or 'thinks'?

AI doesn't 'think' or 'feel' like a human. It's a powerful tool that uses patterns from vast amounts of data to generate responses. You're the director, not just the audience.

✔️ Fact-Check Everything

AI can sound very confident, even when it's wrong. Can you think of a situation where trusting AI without verifying could lead to a problem?

Always verify critical information, especially for important decisions, health, or financial advice. AI can sometimes 'hallucinate' or provide incorrect details that sound convincing.

🔒 Prioritize Privacy

What kind of information should you avoid putting into a public AI tool?

Never share sensitive personal details (like your full address, bank details, or confidential work documents) with public AI tools. Treat it like a public forum where anything you type could be seen or used.
