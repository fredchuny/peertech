üîå Hardware & Power

  • Power Button: Press once to turn on. To turn off, click Start Menu > Power > Shut Down.
  • Charging: If the light near the plug is orange/red, it needs power. Green/white means it is full.
  • The Trackpad:
    • Left Click: Used to click buttons or select things.
    • Right Click: Opens a list of "extra options" (like Copy, Paste, or Delete).
  • USB Ports: Rectangular holes on the side for plugging in a mouse or a USB Storage Stick (Flash Drive) to save files.

üñ•Ô∏è The Interface

  • The Desktop: The main screen you see when you log in. Think of it as your digital surface where you keep your most-used folders.
  • Taskbar: The long bar at the bottom. It shows which apps are currently open.
  • The Start Button: The icon in the bottom-left corner. It is the "Master List" of every app on the computer.
  • Wi-Fi: Click the "Signal" icon near the clock to choose your internet connection.
  • Minimize, Maximize, Close: ‚Äî hides the window, ‚ñ¢ makes it full screen, X closes the app.

üåê The Internet & Bookmarks

  • The Browser: Use Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge to view websites.
  • The Address Bar: The long white bar at the top for typing website names (e.g., google.com).
  • How to Bookmark a Site:
    To save a website you like, click the Star Icon (‚≠ê) on the right side of the address bar. This adds it to your "Favorites" list so you don't have to type the address next time!

üìÅ Files & Folders

  • Downloads: A folder where things go automatically when you save them from the internet.
  • How to Delete a File:
    1. Click the file once to select it.
    2. Press the Delete key on your keyboard, OR Right-click and choose the Trash Can icon or the word "Delete."
  • The Recycle Bin: This is where deleted files sit. If you deleted something by mistake, open this on the Desktop to get it back!

üìä Typing Speed Reference

When using MonkeyType.com, here is how to understand your score (WPM = Words Per Minute):
  • 0 - 20 WPM: Beginner (Still learning where the keys are).
  • 21 - 40 WPM: Average (Comfortable for casual emailing and notes).
  • 41 - 60 WPM: Professional (Good speed for office work).
  • 60+ WPM: High Speed (Very fast!).

üì∑ Camera & Video Calls

  • The Camera: The tiny glass circle at the top of your screen.
  • Privacy Shutter: Check if a tiny plastic slider is covering the lens if the video is black.
  • Test it: Click Start and type "Camera".

üìû Joining a Video Call

  • The Link: Click the blue link in your email or calendar.
  • Microphone Icon: If it has a red line, you are Muted.
  • Camera Icon: If it has a red line, your Video is off.
  • Leaving: Look for the red "Leave" or "End" button.
