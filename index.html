Recognizing Scams

Protect yourself from online fraud and malicious links.

üìß Email & Text Scams (Phishing)

❯
  • **Sense of Urgency:** Scammers often rush you ("Act now or your account will be closed!").
  • **Unexpected Attachments:** Never open an attachment from an email you don't trust.
  • **Grammar/Spelling Errors:** Official communication rarely has typos.
  • **Requests for Money/Gift Cards:** Legitimate services will not ask for payment in this way.

üìû Phone Scams (Vishing)

❯
  • **Impersonation:** Someone claiming to be from the CRA, a bank, or police.
  • **The Threat:** They may threaten arrest or immediate financial penalty.
  • **Rule:** Hang up and call the official organization's number (from their website, not the scammer's number).

üîí Data Privacy Game

❯

Practice recognizing privacy risks by playing the Data Dealer game (5 mins): https://fredchuny.github.io/peertech/data_dealer.html

üí° Practice & Q&A

❯

Question 1: Your bank emails you saying your account will be locked in one hour. What should you do?

❯

Answer: **DO NOT** click any link in the email. Close the email and call your bank using the phone number listed on the back of your card or their official website.
