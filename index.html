Protecting Yourself: Passwords

Why Strong Passwords Matter

They are the first line of defense in protecting your personal information, your email, and your accounts from unauthorized access.

How to Create Strong Passwords

  • Use a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols (like !, @, #).
  • Make them long (at least 8-12 characters is a good start).
  • Use unique passwords for each important account (email, banking).
  • Avoid using personal information that's easy to guess (birthdays, names, pet's names).

Rule #1: Never share your passwords with anyone!

Recognizing Scams & Staying Private

🎣 Phishing & Online Scams

❯
  • In emails/texts, look for bad grammar, urgent requests for money, and suspicious links.
  • Be wary of unsolicited calls asking for personal information or payment.
  • Rule of thumb: If an offer seems too good to be true, it probably is.
  • Always verify: If you're unsure about a message, contact the organization directly using their official website or phone number, not the one in the message.

🔒 Privacy Settings & Public Wi-Fi

❯
  • Understand what information you are sharing on apps and websites. Take a moment to check the privacy settings on social media.
  • Avoid sensitive activities like banking or logging into important accounts when using public, unsecured Wi-Fi.
  • Remember to install software updates, as they often include important security fixes.

💡 Practice & Q&A

❯

Let's consider some safety scenarios:

Question 1: You receive an email from your bank asking you to click a link to "verify your account immediately." What should you do?

❯

Answer: Do NOT click the link. Instead, go directly to your bank's official website (by typing the address yourself) or call them using a trusted phone number to check if there's an issue.

Exercise: Think about a time you might use public Wi-Fi. What kind of online activity would you AVOID doing there?

❯

Hint: Anything that involves your personal or financial information, like online banking or logging into email.
